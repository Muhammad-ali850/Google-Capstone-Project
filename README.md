# Employee Turnover Prediction for HR

## Project Overview

This repository contains my capstone project completed as part of the **Google Advanced Data Analytics Professional Certificate** on Coursera. The project applies data analytics, statistics, and machine learning techniques to solve a real-world Human Resources (HR) business problem: identifying the factors that influence employee turnover and predicting which employees are most likely to leave the company.

The analysis was conducted on an employee dataset provided by **Salifort Motors**, a fictional consulting company seeking to improve employee retention and satisfaction.

## Business Problem

The objective of this project was to:

* Analyze employee behavior and workplace trends.
* Identify key factors associated with employee turnover.
* Build machine learning models capable of predicting employee departures.
* Provide data-driven recommendations to improve employee retention.

## Key Findings

The analysis revealed several strong indicators of employee turnover:

* Employees handling excessive numbers of projects were significantly more likely to leave.
* Higher monthly working hours were associated with lower job satisfaction.
* Employees with low satisfaction levels showed a much higher likelihood of attrition.
* Long working hours, high workloads, and limited promotion opportunities appear to contribute to employee burnout.
  
## Model Performance

### Decision Tree

* Accuracy: 97.8%
* Precision: 95.5%
* Recall: 91.5%
* F1-Score: 93.5%
* ROC-AUC: 97.4%

### Random Forest

* Accuracy: 96.1%
* Precision: 97.7%
* Recall: 78.5%
* F1-Score: 86.8%
* ROC-AUC: 98.2%

The Decision Tree model demonstrated the strongest overall balance of performance metrics.
